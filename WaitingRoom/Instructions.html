Welcome to the experiment. Please pay close attention to the instructions.

You will play a number of rounds of the same game. The basic payment is $4. On top of your basic payment, you can accumulate a bonus payment over the course of the rounds. Your decisions and those of the other, anonymous participants in the experiment will determine the sum of your money (a detailed explanation is provided below).

You will play the game in groups of 5 players. The 4 players that join your group will stay the same over all rounds that you play.

In each round, you will choose one of 4 buttons, labeled A, B, C, and D. You can earn $0.25 additional bonus if no other player chooses the button you chose. However, you do not earn any bonus if one or more other participants in your group also chose the button you chose in this round.

Every player will be given a unique player number, which stays the same over all rounds. As you complete rounds, you will see the choice history of the last 8 rounds and accumulated earnings of all players.

For each choice, you have 60 seconds, which you can extend a maximum of twice by 30 seconds. The total possible time per choice is 120 seconds. If you do not make a choice in that time, the computer will make a random choice for you.

You will play up to 20 rounds or up to 30 minutes after the invited time, whichever occurs first.

To help you understand how the game works, here is an example from Round 10



  • On this page, you will see a task with multiple buttons representing different choices (e.g., A, B, C, D).
  • When you click on a button, a new button will appear showing the option you selected.
  • You can change your choice before confirming your choice.
  • You must confirm your choice to proceed to the next page (i.e., the next round).

You can see a section called Recent History. This shows the choices made by all participants in your group in the previous rounds, in order by player number. Some of them are in brackets, which means they are selected by more than one person in that round.

In addition, an amount of money is shown next to each player. This reflects the accumulated bonus earned by each player up to (but not including) the current round, which is round 3 now.

Note: The Recent History panel always displays information for the last 8 completed rounds.

This information is provided to help you track patterns and performance within your group as the game progresses.
