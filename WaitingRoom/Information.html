(1) What is this study about?
You are invited to take part in a research study about a coordination game. You have been invited to participate in this study because you are an active user of Prolific. The information on this page tells you about the research study. Knowing what is involved will help you decide if you want to take part in the study. Please read this page carefully and ask questions about anything that you don’t understand or want to know more about.

Participation in this research study is voluntary.

(2) Who is running the study?
The study is being carried out by the following researcher(s): [Ommitted]

(3) What will the study involve for me?
The study involves playing a number of coordination games with other participants. You will be able to answer these from your computer at home. The study will be conducted anonymously, we will not ask you for your name or any other identifying information.

(4) How much of my time will the study take?
Completing the experiment will take about 30 minutes of your time.

(5) Do I have to be in the study? Can I withdraw from the study once I have started?
Being in this study is completely voluntary and you do not have to take part. Your decision whether to participate will not affect your current or future relationship with the researchers or anyone else at the University of East Anglia now or in the future.

If you decide to take part in the study, you can withdraw at any point by closing the browser tab and clicking ``Stop Without Completing'' on Prolific.

(6) What are the consequences if I withdraw from the study?
If you decide to take part in the study and then change your mind, you are free to withdraw at any time before you have submitted your decisions. Once you have submitted them, your responses cannot be withdrawn because they are anonymous and therefore we will not be able to tell which one is yours.

(7) Are there any risks or costs associated with being in the study?
Aside from giving up your time, we do not expect that there will be any risks or costs associated with taking part in this study.

(8) Are there any benefits associated with being in the study?
By participating, you help us and the academic community to better understand coordination behaviour.

(9) What will happen to information provided by me and data collected during the study?
Your personal data and information will only be used as outlined on this page, unless you consent otherwise. Data management will follow the Data Protection Act 2018 (DPA 2018) and UK General Data Protection Regulation (UK GDPR), and the University of East Anglia's Research Data Management Policy.

The information you provide will be stored securely and your identity will be kept strictly confidential, except as required by law. Study findings may be published and may also be used for other scholarly and educational purposes such as in teaching, but you will not be identified if you decide to participate in this study. The data will be kept for at least 10 years beyond the last date the data were used. The study findings may be deposited in a repository to allow it to facilitate its reuse. The deposited data will not include your name or any identifiable information about you.
